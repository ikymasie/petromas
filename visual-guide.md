This visual design guideline is crafted to preserve the professional, industrial, and "clean energy" aesthetic established in your current website drafts. The goal is to balance the reliability of a petroleum company with the forward-thinking energy of a modern tech firm.

---

## 1. Brand Color Palette

The brand relies on a high-contrast relationship between a deep, "Trust" Navy and a vibrant, "Growth" Lime Green.

| Use Case | Color Name | Hex Code | Visual Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary** | Deep Petrol Navy | `#2D2A77` | Used for logos, primary buttons, and headings. |
| **Secondary** | Energy Lime | `#82C341` | Used for accents, icons, and "petro" text. |
| **Background** | Off-White / Sky | `#F8F9FA` | Light grey-white for section backgrounds to reduce eye strain. |
| **Neutral** | Charcoal Grey | `#333333` | Used for body text to ensure maximum readability. |

---

## 2. Typography Strategy

To maintain the industrial-modern look, use clean **Sans-Serif** fonts.

* **Headings (H1, H2):** Use a bold, wide Sans-Serif (like **Montserrat** or **Poppins**). All-caps styling for section headers (e.g., "ON-DEMAND FUEL DELIVERY") adds an authoritative, industrial feel.
* **Body Text:** Use a highly legible font (like **Inter** or **Open Sans**) at a minimum size of 16px.
* **Accent Text:** Use the Energy Lime color for sub-headers or "Core Features" labels to guide the eye.

---

## 3. Visual Element & Iconography

Inspired by your current "About Us" and "Services" sections:

* **Icon Style:** Use **Thin-Line Icons** in Energy Lime. They should be simple, geometric, and surrounded by plenty of white space.
* **The "Flame" Graphic:** The logo's flame motif should be used as a subtle watermark or decorative element in section corners to reinforce brand identity.
* **Data Visualization:** For stats (like the "2,500+ Customer Visits"), use large, outlined (stroke) fonts for the numbers to make them feel modern and airy rather than heavy.

---

## 4. Imagery & Layout Principles

Based on your screenshots, the design should follow these "Industrial-Premium" rules:

* **Hero Imagery:** Use high-resolution photos of workers in the field (safety gear visible) or industrial landscapes at "Golden Hour" (sunset/sunrise). This adds a human element and warmth to a technical industry.
* **Shape Language:** Incorporate **Circle Masks** for secondary images (like the technician on the delivery page) to break up the rigid grid of the website.
* **Call to Action (CTA):** Primary buttons should be Deep Petrol Navy with white text. For a "hover" state, transition the background to Energy Lime.
* **The Global Map:** Use a dark-themed, simplified world map with lime green "pulsing" hotspots to indicate your reach without cluttering the screen.

---

## 5. UI Components Guidance

> **Note on Navigation:** Keep the header clean. A white background with the logo on the left and a dedicated, high-contrast "CONTACT US" button on the far right ensures the user always knows how to take the next step.

* **Accordions:** For the "Fuel Delivery" section, use clean white boxes with thin grey borders. Use a "+" or "–" in Energy Lime to indicate interactivity.
* **Whitespace:** Don’t be afraid of it. High-end industrial brands use large margins to signify "quality" and "precision."
